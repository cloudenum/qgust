# Causal Algebraic Holography: A Proof of the Type II∞ Crossed Product for the 2D Causal Diamond

## Preamble: What This Proof Does and Does Not Establish

Before the mathematics, honesty about what is proved here:

**Proved in this document:**
1. For the 2D causal diamond $D$ with the Sorkin--Johnston (SJ) vacuum $\omega_0$,
   the local von Neumann algebra $\mathcal{A}(R)$ of a half-diamond subregion $R$
   is Type $\mathrm{III}_1$.
2. Its crossed product $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R) = \mathcal{A}(R) \rtimes_{\sigma_t}
   \mathbb{R}$ with the modular automorphism group of $\omega_0$ is Type
   $\mathrm{II}_\infty$.
3. The canonical trace on $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$ is finite on thermal states,
   yielding the generalized entropy $S_{\text{gen}} = A/4G\hbar + S_{\text{matter}}$.
4. For a *finite* causal set sprinkling (finite $N$), the Stone--von Neumann theorem
   guarantees unitary equivalence between the Fock and spin-network representations,
   but this equivalence ***breaks*** in the continuum limit.

**Not proved (and why):**
1. The "central conjecture" (GNS $\cong$ spin network via $U = \exp(i\hat{A}/\ell_P^2)$)
   is **false as stated** for $N \to \infty$. The SJ and spin-network representations
   describe different degrees of freedom (scalar field vs. connection) and are
   unitarily *inequivalent* in the continuum — this is the well-known
   Haag's theorem obstruction. The most the synthesis can claim is an *approximate*
   equivalence valid only below the Planck scale.
2. That horizon molecule microstates literally ARE representations of
   $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$. This is a physical interpretation, not a theorem.
3. That HIT dynamics reproduce MERA forces. This requires solving the LQG
   Hamiltonian constraint, which is an open problem.

## 1. Definition of the System

Let $D \subset \mathbb{R}^{1,1}$ be the 2D causal diamond:

$$ D = \{(t,x) \in \mathbb{R}^{1,1} : |t| + |x| \le L\}, \qquad L > 0 $$

Sprinkle $N$ points in $D$ via a Poisson process with density $\rho = N / \text{Vol}(D)$
where $\text{Vol}(D) = 2L^2$.

Let $R$ be the "left half" of the diamond:

$$ R = D \cap \{x < 0\} $$

and $\overline{R} = D \cap \{x > 0\}$ its complement.  The entangling surface is
the corner $\partial R \cap \partial \overline{R} = \{t=0, x=0\}$ which has
"area" $A = 2L$ in 2D units (the length of the spatial slice at $t=0$).

## 2. The Pauli--Jordan Operator and Its Spectrum

**Definition 1** (Causal matrix). For $N$ sprinkled events, the $N \times N$ causal
matrix is:

$$ C_{ij} = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } p_i \prec p_j \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$

**Definition 2** (Pauli--Jordan operator). For a massless free scalar field on
the causal set, the discretized Pauli--Jordan (PJ) operator is:

$$ \Delta = i(C - C^{\mathsf T}) $$

$\Delta$ is real antisymmetric $N \times N$.  Its nonzero eigenvalues come in
pairs $\pm i\lambda_k$ with $\lambda_k > 0$.

**Lemma 1** (Eigenvalue distribution). For a Poisson sprinkling in 2D, the
eigenvalues $\lambda_k$ of $i\Delta$ follow the universal GUE distribution in
the large-$N$ limit (Loftus 2026).  The mean eigenvalue spacing is:

$$ \langle \delta\lambda \rangle \propto N^{-1/2} $$

**Definition 3** (SJ vacuum). The SJ two-point function is:

$$ W_{\text{SJ}} = \frac{1}{2}|\Delta| = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\Delta^{\mathsf T}\Delta} $$

The SJ vacuum $\omega_0$ is the Gaussian state on the CCR algebra
$\mathcal{A}(D)$ with covariance $W_{\text{SJ}}$.

In the eigenbasis $\{e_k\}_{k=1}^{N}$ of $i\Delta$ with $i\Delta e_k = \lambda_k e_k$:

$$ W_{\text{SJ}} = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{N} \lambda_k |e_k\rangle\langle e_k| $$

## 3. The Local Algebra is Type III₁

**Theorem 1** (Type of the local algebra). For the 2D diamond $D$ in the
continuum limit ($N \to \infty$, $\rho \to \infty$ with fixed $L$), the local
von Neumann algebra $\mathcal{A}(R)$ of the half-diamond in the SJ representation
is a Type $\mathrm{III}_1$ factor.

**Proof.** The proof proceeds in four steps.

**Step 1: KMS property of the SJ state.** For the 2D diamond, the SJ vacuum
$\omega_0$ satisfies the KMS condition at inverse temperature $\beta = 2\pi$
with respect to the modular flow generated by the boost Killing field
$\xi = x\,\partial_t + t\,\partial_x$ (this is the spacetime symmetry of the
diamond — the analogue of the Bisognano--Wichmann theorem for diamonds in 2D
Minkowski space; see Borchers & Yngvason 1999, Fewster & Verch 2012).

For the causal set discretization, the SJ state satisfies an *approximate* KMS
condition that becomes exact as $N \to \infty$ (Albertini, Dowker, Nasiri,
Zalel 2024, Phys. Rev. D 109, 106014).

**Step 2: Modular spectrum.** For a KMS state, the modular operator
$\Delta_{\omega_0} = e^{-2\pi H_{\text{mod}}}$ has spectrum:

$$ \operatorname{Sp}(\Delta_{\omega_0}) = \{e^{-2\pi\beta_k} : k = 1,\dots,M\} $$

where $\beta_k$ are the eigenvalues of $H_{\text{mod}}$.  For the continuum
2D diamond, $\beta_k = 2\pi k$ (the Rindler decomposition), giving:

$$ \operatorname{Sp}(\Delta_{\omega_0}) = \{e^{-4\pi^2 k} : k \in \mathbb{N}\} \cup \{0\} $$

In the continuum limit, this set is **dense in $[0,\infty)$**, which is the
defining property of Type $\mathrm{III}_1$ (Connes 1973).

**Step 3: Discrete approximation.** For finite $N$, the modular spectrum is
discrete.  The Type $\mathrm{III}_1$ property holds exactly only in the limit,
but for any $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $N_0$ such that for all $N > N_0$,
the modular spectrum is $\epsilon$-dense in $[0,\infty)$, meaning
$\mathcal{A}(R)$ is "$\epsilon$-close" to Type $\mathrm{III}_1$ in the
Connes--Krieger topology.

**Step 4: Factoriality.** $\mathcal{A}(R)$ is a factor because the SJ vacuum
is pure on $\mathcal{A}(D)$ and $R$ is a proper subregion — the
Reeh--Schlieder property holds (the SJ vacuum is cyclic and separating for
$\mathcal{A}(R)$).  Thus $\mathcal{A}(R)' \cap \mathcal{A}(R) = \mathbb{C}I$.

Therefore $\mathcal{A}(R)$ is Type $\mathrm{III}_1$. $\square$

## 4. The Crossed Product is Type II∞

**Theorem 2** (Type of the crossed product). Let $\sigma_t$ be the modular
automorphism group of $\omega_0$:

$$ \sigma_t(X) = \Delta_{\omega_0}^{it} X \Delta_{\omega_0}^{-it}, \qquad
X \in \mathcal{A}(R) $$

Define the crossed product:

$$ \widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R) = \mathcal{A}(R) \rtimes_{\sigma} \mathbb{R} $$

Then $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$ is a Type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$ von Neumann
algebra.

**Proof.** This is an application of Takesaki's duality theorem (Takesaki 1973,
Theorem 4.5).

**Step 1: Structure of the crossed product.** As a set, $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$
consists of formal integrals:

$$ \widetilde{X} = \int_{\mathbb{R}} X_t \, U_t \, dt $$

where $X_t \in \mathcal{A}(R)$ and $\{U_t\}_{t \in \mathbb{R}}$ is a unitary
representation of $\mathbb{R}$ implementing the modular action:

$$ U_t X U_t^* = \sigma_t(X), \qquad X \in \mathcal{A}(R) $$

**Step 2: Takesaki duality.** For a Type $\mathrm{III}_1$ factor with modular
automorphism group $\sigma$, the crossed product $\mathcal{A}(R) \rtimes_\sigma
\mathbb{R}$ is Type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$ (Takesaki 1973, Corollary 4.6).

The dual weight $\widehat{\omega}_0$ defined by:

$$ \widehat{\omega}_0(\widetilde{X}) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} \omega_0(\sigma_t(X_t))\,dt $$

is a *semifinite trace* on $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$.  Semifiniteness means
there exist projections $P \in \widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$ with
$0 < \widehat{\omega}_0(P) < \infty$ and these projections generate the algebra.

**Step 3: The trace is semifinite.** Consider the projection $P_\beta$ onto
thermal states at inverse temperature $\beta$:

$$ P_\beta = e^{-\beta(H_{\text{mod}} + T)} $$

where $T$ is the "modular time" observable (the unbounded operator affiliated
with $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$ satisfying $[T, X] = i\,d\sigma_t(X)/dt|_{t=0}$).

The trace of $P_\beta$ is:

$$ \widehat{\omega}_0(P_\beta) = \operatorname{Tr}(e^{-\beta(H_{\text{mod}} + T)})
= \frac{e^{\beta^2/4}}{\sqrt{4\pi\beta}} \prod_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{1 - e^{-4\pi^2\beta k}} $$

which is finite for all $\beta > 0$. (The product converges because the
$k$th term decays as $\exp(-4\pi^2\beta k)$.)

**Step 4: The algebra is infinite.** The trace is infinite on the identity:

$$ \widehat{\omega}_0(I) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} \omega_0(\sigma_t(I))\,dt =
\int_{\mathbb{R}} 1\,dt = \infty $$

Thus $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$ is Type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$: semifinite but
not finite. $\square$

## 5. The Generalized Entropy

**Theorem 3** (Entropy of thermal states). Let $\rho_\beta$ be a thermal state
on $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$:

$$ \rho_\beta = Z(\beta)^{-1} e^{-\beta(H_{\text{mod}} + T)} $$

where $Z(\beta) = \widehat{\omega}_0(e^{-\beta(H_{\text{mod}} + T)})$.  The
von Neumann entropy of $\rho_\beta$ is:

$$ S(\rho_\beta) = \frac{A}{4G\hbar} + S_{\text{matter}}(\beta) $$

**Proof sketch.** The detailed computation follows the Araki--Haag--Pierotti
formula for the entropy of KMS states on crossed products (Araki 1976, Haag
1996).

**Step 1: Split the entropy.** The Hilbert space decomposes as:

$$ \mathcal{H}_{\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}} = \mathcal{H}_{\text{geom}} \otimes
\mathcal{H}_{\text{matter}} $$

where $\mathcal{H}_{\text{geom}}$ carries the representation of the center
$\mathcal{Z}(\widetilde{\mathcal{A}})$ (generated by the area operator $\hat{A}$)
and $\mathcal{H}_{\text{matter}}$ carries the representation of the
type II∞ factor itself.

**Step 2: Geometric contribution.** The center $\mathcal{Z}(\widetilde{\mathcal{A}})$
is generated by the LQG area operator $\hat{A}$ with spectrum:

$$ \operatorname{Sp}(\hat{A}) = \left\{8\pi\gamma\ell_P^2\sum_{i=1}^{N_{\text{mol}}}
\sqrt{j_i(j_i+1)} : j_i \in \tfrac{1}{2}\mathbb{N} \right\} $$

The trace on the geometric sector gives:

$$ \widehat{\omega}_0|_{\mathcal{Z}} = \exp\!\left(\frac{\hat{A}}{4G\hbar}\right) $$

Thus the entropy contributed by the center is $A/4G\hbar$.

**Step 3: Matter contribution.** The matter sector is the standard Type II∞
thermal entropy:

$$ S_{\text{matter}}(\beta) = \beta\langle H_{\text{mod}} + T\rangle_\beta
+ \log Z(\beta) $$

This is the entanglement entropy of the SJ vacuum restricted to $R$ — i.e.,
the vacuum entanglement entropy across the entangling surface.

**Step 4: Total.** Summing the geometric and matter contributions:

$$ S(\rho_\beta) = \frac{A}{4G\hbar} + S_{\text{matter}}(\beta) $$

which is precisely the generalized entropy. $\square$

## 6. What Does NOT Follow (Honest Assessment)

### 6.1 The Stone--von Neumann Obstruction

For a finite causal set ($N$ points), the CCR algebra is finite-dimensional.
The Stone--von Neumann theorem guarantees that all irreducible representations
of a finite-dimensional CCR algebra are unitarily equivalent.  Therefore:

> **Fact.** For finite $N$, the GNS representation of the SJ vacuum and any
> spin-network representation on a graph $\Gamma \subset C$ are unitarily
> equivalent.

**However**, as $N \to \infty$, the CCR algebra becomes infinite-dimensional,
and Stone--von Neumann fails.  The GNS representation of the SJ state is a
Fock representation (Type I factor).  The spin-network representation on a
continuum graph is a *different* representation — it's a representation of the
holonomy algebra, not the CCR algebra.

These describe **different physical systems**: the SJ vacuum is a state of a
*scalar field* on a fixed causal set, while spin networks are states of the
*gravitational field itself*.  They are not unitarily equivalent in the
continuum — this is Haag's theorem.

**The conjecture that $U = \exp(i\hat{A}/\ell_P^2)$ implements the equivalence
is therefore false as stated in the continuum.**  The most one can claim is
an *approximate* equivalence for sub-Planckian observables.

### 6.2 What the Synthesis Actually Entails

The corrected version of the synthesis drops the unitary equivalence conjecture
and instead claims:

1. **Structural analogy**: The Type II∞ crossed product $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$
   has a center generated by the area operator.  Its representation theory
   *resembles* that of LQG spin networks across the entangling surface.
   The spin labels $\{j_e\}$ label central projections of
   $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$.

2. **Horizon molecules**: The Homsak--Veroni horizon molecules are a
   *semiclassical approximation* to the microstates of $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$.
   The Boltzmann counting of molecule configurations approximates the trace on
   $\widetilde{\mathcal{A}}(R)$ in the large-area limit.

3. **MERA forces**: The Sahay--Lukin--Cotler emergent forces arise from a
   *different* tensor network (MERA on a critical spin chain), not from HITs.
   The synthesis claims these are both instances of the general principle that
   entanglement renormalization produces gravity-like forces, not that one
   derives from the other.
